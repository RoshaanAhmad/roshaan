For a long time, it was believed that we were born with all the brain cells we'd ever have, but research has revealed that certain areas of the adult brain, like the hippocampus (crucial for learning and memory) and the subventricular zone (involved in generating new cells that migrate to the olfactory bulb), continue to produce new neurones throughout our lives.
